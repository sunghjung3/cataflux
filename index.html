The breakthrough

A foundation model for how materials transform

A recent breakthrough in ML made it possible to simulate atoms a thousand times faster. We used it to build the world's largest dataset of material reactions — then trained a generative AI foundation model that finally makes atomic-scale reaction simulations tractable.

Predicts not just stability, but reaction kinetics and pathways
Simulates how real materials react, transform, and fail — at scale
Trained on 1,000× more reaction data than any comparable dataset
Built on decades of physics expertise in reaction mechanisms

Applications

Replacing the most expensive part of materials R&D

Every materials company in the world is bottlenecked by the lab. We're not competing for a slice of computational chemistry — we're replacing the wet bench itself.

01

Catalyst design

Predict which catalyst surfaces and compositions will actually work before synthesizing them — accelerating discovery from years to weeks.

02

Battery development

Simulate electrode degradation, ion transport, and interface reactions to design batteries that last longer and charge faster.

03

Semiconductor manufacturing

Model defect formation and diffusion in semiconductor materials to optimize fabrication processes and improve yields.

04

Corrosion prevention

Predict how alloys degrade in real environments — enabling the design of materials that resist corrosion from the start.
